Barna Research Findings

1. Youth Kids like Jesus


Around the world, youth kids think of Jesus as trustworthy, generous, wise, peaceful.

2. But dont feel the same about Christians


Christians are viewed less as loving, less wise, much less sincere, more judgmental and especially hypocritical compared to Jesus.

3. Passionate about justice and activism


Today’s teens are zealous toward justice and want to see change in the world and can see themselves being part of it.

4. Teens respect the Bible


Teens respect the Bible and are curious about its contents, but not sure how scripture can inform and influence the world around them.

5. Partial knowledge of Gospel


Teens only know a partial Gospel. 48% of Christian youth believe Jesus is God.

6. Teens want to know Gospel is good


51% of Christian youth kids want to see Jesus teaching as promoting good in world.

5 adults to 1 youth kid


Young people who could name 5 or more non-parental adult mentors who know them and love them unconditionally continued to grow in their faith through college and beyond and are less likely to leave the church.



What are helpful strategies for youth group kids today?

Teach that Jesus is truth.


Teach them the Gospel, provide apologetic evidences, answer all questions.

Gospel as good for the world.


Example of Jesus as trustworthy, generous, wise, peaceful is good for the world and is needed now.

Get 5 adults for 1 youth kid.


More adults show that youth kids are more likely to read the bible and pray Continue attending church after college and beyond.

Teach them Christians are sinners.


People are broken sinners and we need a savior. Our hope is Jesus not man.
